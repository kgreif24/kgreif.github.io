I'm a PhD student in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at the University of California, Irvine. My research is in applications of AI to particle physics, in particular to the ATLAS experiment at the Large Hadron Collider. I'm interested in AI for natural science, scientific foundation models, and any tough problem that can be solved with lots of data. I am best reached via email at kgreif@uci.edu.

Research Interests

  • De-correlation (aka "unfolding") in particle physics experiments: This is a problem that arises when trying to extract physical quantities from data that has been affected by detector effects. I use AI methods to statistically correct for detector effects and extract the physical quantities of interest.
  • Scientific foundation models: I'm interested in building large scale AI models specifically for the types of data found in fundamental physics experiments, in the hope that they can unlock entirely new classes of measurements and insights.
  • Jets and QCD in collider physics experiments: The strong nuclear force is responsible for creating jets of particles in proton-proton collisions at the LHC. I'm interested in using AI and other statistical methods to study these objects in their native high-dimensionality.
